My name is Henry Schiller.

I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in the Department of Philosophy at King's College London.

henry.schiller [at] kcl.ac.uk

Before coming to KCL in I had postdocs at the University of Vienna, the University of Sheffield, and Washington University in St. Louis. I did my PhD at UT Austin with Josh Dever and Mark Sainsbury. My dissertation was about communication and rationality.

Right now I'm working on a paper about the difference between rational learning and arational attitude change (r&r at Mind) and I'm writing a survey article about speech acts and rational attitude change for Philosophy Compass.

I have papers under review about the function of folk psychology (r&r at Mind and Language), the rationality of hoarding rather than updating on information you learn (with Jake Quilty-Dunn), and the rational acquisition of social rules (with Shaun Nichols).
